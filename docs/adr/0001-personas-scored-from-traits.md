# Personas are scored from Traits, not from creativity patterns

Early copy described four "creativity patterns" (logic, design, leadership, expression) that answers would be matched to. The eight authored Personas do not fit that model: Loop Legend (persistent, patient, resilient) and Cool Dev (innovative, bold, curious) fall outside all four buckets, and Binary Babe and Debug Master both land squarely on logic. Rather than cut or distort the Personas to fit an invented framework, we retired the pattern layer and score answers directly toward Traits, matching a Persona by its Trait signature.

## Considered Options

- **Six patterns** — add *grit* and *rebellion* to cover Loop Legend and Cool Dev. Rejected: still forces two Personas per pattern on an uneven grid, and the pattern layer earns nothing that Traits don't already do.
- **Four personas** — collapse the roster to match the four patterns. Rejected: throws away the strongest names and makes one in four students a twin, killing the share value the leaderboard depends on.

## Consequences

The "four creativity patterns" promise in the landing copy is no longer true and must be reworded to speak about Traits. The Trait vocabulary inherited from the Persona descriptions is now load-bearing and needs to be a deliberate, shared set rather than free-text adjectives.
