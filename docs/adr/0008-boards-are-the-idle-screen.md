# The boards are the idle screen, and the Reveal times out

MEI MEI runs as a kiosk on one laptop, so the app's resting state is the Faction Board and Rank Board rather than a landing page with a start button. The boards are the only recruiting surface left: there are no phones, no second screen, and no sharing beyond a photograph, so a screen showing live Team standings is what makes a student walk over to the booth — a dormant START button is not. The loop is: boards (idle) → START → Name → Persona Round → Knowledge Round → Reveal → boards with the new entry highlighted → idle.

## Consequences

The Reveal returns to the boards after **60 seconds of inactivity**. Students photograph their Reveal and walk away without dismissing it — constantly — and without the timeout the next student sits down facing the previous student's Name, Persona and Score, and starts the quiz as them.

Both boards share one screen and must read as visibly separate things, or students take their Persona as a verdict rather than a match (ADR-0006). The Faction Board shows eight Team bars; the Rank Board shows the top 10 Names with Scores.

Putting Names on a public screen reopens impersonation and profanity: the Rank Board needs a length cap on Name and a fast way for booth staff to delete a row. The Reveal is the only private moment in the loop, so a student's full Score belongs there — the board only ever shows the top 10.
