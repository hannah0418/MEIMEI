# The board ranks Teams, not students

The landing copy promised students they could "climb the leaderboard", but a Persona match is not an achievement — there are no right answers, and two students cannot be meaningfully compared. Every way of ranking students individually damages something: scoring needs correct answers and kills the premise, timing rewards mashing and corrupts the answers we match on, and activity-based ranking rewards retakes that make the Persona data worthless. So the board ranks Teams by member count, and a student climbs by recruiting — sharing their Persona so friends take the quiz and their Team's count rises.

## Consequences

The share loop becomes the core mechanic rather than a step-04 afterthought, and gaming is pointless because a retake only moves you between Teams. No individual student ever sees their own name at the top; if personal glory is later judged non-negotiable, that needs an honestly earnable badge system with its own anti-abuse work, not a revival of scoring. Board integrity now rests entirely on counting each student once — which makes identity load-bearing.
