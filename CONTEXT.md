# MEI MEI

A creativity quiz for the College of Computer Studies, run from one laptop at a department org fair. Students answer questions about how they code, design, lead, and debug, are matched to a named creative identity with a P-pop stage-name flavour, and then prove their CS knowledge for a place on the rankings.

The quiz is two Rounds because matching and scoring cannot share a question: an option that is *correct* stops being an *honest* answer, and the Persona data converges on whatever the room thinks is virtuous.

## Language

**Persona**:
One of eight named creative identities a student can be matched to. The authoritative unit of the domain — everything the student sees, shares, and collects is expressed in terms of a Persona.
_Avoid_: type, result, archetype, character

**Trait**:
A single creative characteristic that answers score toward. Traits are the scoring dimension and are shared across Personas — every Trait feeds two or more Personas, so no Trait is a rename of a Persona. A Persona is identified by the Traits it scores highest on.
_Avoid_: attribute, dimension, axis, stat

**Descriptor**:
One of the three adjectives printed on a Persona's reveal card ("Playful, Fun-loving, Energetic"). Descriptors are what the student reads; Traits are what the quiz scores. A Descriptor never affects matching.
_Avoid_: trait, tag, label

**Persona Round**:
The first Round: twelve questions about how the student works, with no correct answers, which produce the Persona. Nothing in this Round can be right or wrong — that is what keeps the answers honest.
_Avoid_: round 1, personality quiz, part one

**Knowledge Round**:
The second Round: IT/CS questions that have real answers, which produce the Score. The only Round that can be won.
_Avoid_: round 2, trivia, part two, the hard part

**Score**:
How many Knowledge Round questions a student got right. Never touches Persona matching.
_Avoid_: points, result, rating

**Name**:
What the student types before starting. Self-declared and unverified — it identifies a Response for display, not a person for the system.
_Avoid_: username, player, account, student ID

**Response**:
One completed run of both Rounds — the Name, the answers given, the Persona matched, and the Score. The unit the Faction Board counts and the Rank Board ranks.
_Avoid_: submission, entry, attempt, session

**Reveal**:
The full-screen card ending a Response: the student's Name, their Persona with its Descriptors and styling, and their Score. Held until dismissed and never revisited — the student photographs it with their own phone. It is the only thing that leaves the booth.
_Avoid_: result page, result screen, card

**Team**:
All Responses matched to the same Persona. Counted in Responses, not people — the quiz has no accounts, so one student who takes it twice joins two Teams. A Response joins a Team by being matched, never by choosing.
_Avoid_: faction, group, house, squad

**Faction Board**:
The standing of Teams against each other, by Response count. It ranks Personas, never students — a match is not an achievement.
_Avoid_: leaderboard, rankings, scoreboard

**Rank Board**:
Names ranked by Score. The only ranking of students in MEI MEI, and it ranks them on Knowledge Round answers alone — never on their Persona.
_Avoid_: leaderboard, high scores, top players

**Staff Mode**:
The password-protected state where booth staff can delete one Response or clear all Responses. It changes moderation controls, not quiz participation.
_Avoid_: admin panel, manage mode, dashboard

**Creativity Pattern**:
Retired. An earlier four-bucket model (logic, design, leadership, expression) that could not express the eight Personas — half the roster fell outside it. Personas are matched from Traits directly, with no intermediate bucket.
_Avoid_: pattern, category, bucket, quadrant

## The Personas

Every Persona is exactly three Traits, so that a Persona's score can be a plain sum without larger signatures winning by arithmetic.

**Spark UI Designer** — Craft, Rigor, Novelty
_Descriptors_: Creative, Detail-Oriented, Innovative

**Binary Babe** — Analysis, Rigor, Speed
_Descriptors_: Analytical, Precise, Sharp

**Debug Master** — Analysis, Rigor, Composure
_Descriptors_: Analytical, Calm, Methodical

**Diva Scrum Master** — Command, Nerve, Voice
_Descriptors_: Organized, Charismatic, Decisive

**Loop Legend** — Grit, Composure, Rigor
_Descriptors_: Persistent, Patient, Resilient

**Code Cutie** — Play, Voice, Novelty
_Descriptors_: Playful, Fun-loving, Energetic

**Syntax Superstar** — Voice, Nerve, Craft
_Descriptors_: Expressive, Communicative, Bold

**Cool Dev** — Novelty, Nerve, Speed
_Descriptors_: Innovative, Bold, Curious

Binary Babe and Debug Master are the same intellect at opposite tempos — Speed against Composure. That axis is the only thing separating them, and it is load-bearing.

## The Traits

**Rigor**:
Care for correctness and detail in the work itself.
_Absorbs_: Precise, Detail-Oriented, Methodical

**Analysis**:
Breaking a problem into parts and reasoning from them.
_Absorbs_: Analytical

**Craft**:
Attention to how a thing looks and feels to the person using it.
_Absorbs_: Creative

**Novelty**:
Pull toward the new, the unfamiliar, the untried.
_Absorbs_: Innovative, Curious

**Command**:
Taking direction of other people and driving to a decision.
_Absorbs_: Organized, Charismatic, Decisive

**Grit**:
Continuing through repeated failure.
_Absorbs_: Persistent, Resilient

**Composure**:
Staying level under pressure and frustration. The slow half of the tempo axis it shares with Speed.
_Absorbs_: Calm, Patient

**Speed**:
Quickness and incisiveness of judgment — seeing the answer and committing to it. The fast half of the tempo axis it shares with Composure.
_Absorbs_: Sharp

**Play**:
Bringing fun and energy to the work.
_Absorbs_: Playful, Fun-loving, Energetic

**Voice**:
Making the work legible and vivid to other people.
_Absorbs_: Expressive, Communicative

**Nerve**:
Willingness to act or speak against caution.
_Absorbs_: Bold
